🔵 VOZ PASIVA — ABSOLUTAMENTE TODO

📌 Estructura base

Activa: Subject + Verb + Object
Pasiva: Object → Subject + BE + Past Participle

👉 El tiempo verbal SIEMPRE lo marca el verbo BE.

📌 ¿Cuándo se usa?

  • Cuando el agente no importa
  • Cuando no se sabe quién hace la acción
  • Textos formales (noticias, ciencia, exámenes)

🕒 Pasiva por tiempos verbales (COMPLETA)

Tiempo Ejemplo activo Pasiva correcta
Present Simple They clean the streets The streets are cleaned
Past Simple They cleaned the streets The streets were cleaned
Present Continuous They are building a bridge A bridge is being built
Past Continuous They were building a bridge A bridge was being built
Present Perfect They have fixed the car The car has been fixed
Past Perfect They had fixed the car The car had been fixed
Future (will) They will announce the results The results will be announced
Conditional They would solve the issue The issue would be solved
Modal verbs They must finish the work The work must be finished
Modal perfect They should have warned us We should have been warned
❌ NO se usa pasiva con Present Perfect Continuous
(They have been repairing… → NO natural en pasiva)

🟠 CAUSATIVE — HAVE / GET SOMETHING DONE

🕒 Causative por tiempos verbales (COMPLETA)

Tiempo Ejemplo activo Causative correcto
Present Simple The mechanic fixes my car I have my car fixed
Present Continuous The mechanic is fixing my car I am having my car fixed
Past Simple The mechanic fixed my car I had my car fixed
Past Continuous The mechanic was fixing my car I was having my car fixed
Present Perfect The mechanic has fixed my car I have had my car fixed
Past Perfect The mechanic had fixed my car I had had my car fixed
Future (will) The mechanic will fix my car I will have my car fixed
Conditional The mechanic would fix my car I would have my car fixed
Modal verbs The mechanic must fix my car I must have my car fixed
Modal perfect The mechanic should have fixed my car I should have had my car fixed
❌ Importante: En presente perfecto continuo y pasado perfecto continuo no se usa causative naturalmente.
Ejemplo incorrecto: I have been having my car fixed ❌

🟢 RELATIVE CLAUSES — TODO LO QUE CAE

📌 Pronombres

Pronombre Uso Ejemplo
who Personas The man who lives here
which Cosas (non-restrictive) My car, which is new, is electric
that Personas o cosas (restrictive) The exam that we did
whose Posesión The boy whose bike was stolen
where Lugar The city where I live
when Tiempo The day when we met
🧠 Regla de oro:
¿La información es necesaria?
✔️ Sí → THAT
❌ No → COMAS + WHICH / WHO
